The controlled generation of mature stem cell-derived islets (SC-islets) remains a barrier to scalable cell 16 

therapy for diabetes. Here, we develop a predictive digital model defining the cell-state-specific regulatory 17 

logic governing fate specification during human SC-islet differentiation. We integrate 400,603 cells from 9 18 

original single-cell multiomic datasets and 52 public single-cell RNA-seq and ATAC-seq datasets across 4 19 

cell lines and 7 differentiation protocols. This model resolves transcriptional and chromatin accessibility 20 

dynamics while enabling time-resolved inference and in silico perturbation of cell-state-specific gene 21 

regulatory networks. We identify regulators across trajectories from endoderm progenitors to pancreatic 22 

exocrine and endocrine lineages, nominating new candidate regulators. Among these candidates, we 23 

validate previously unreported roles for STAT1 as an exocrine driver and ZEB1 as a dynamic regulator of 24 

early endocrine specification and later off-target serotonergic islet cell fate. This work provides an 25 

experimentally supported predictive framework and an interactive resource comprising 1,116 simulations 26 

to prioritize transcription factors and intervention windows for refining SC-islet differentiation. 27

INTRODUCTION 33 

Recent clinical trials have demonstrated that stem cell-derived islets (SC-islets) can meaningfully restore 34 

glucose regulation in people living with type 1 diabetes 1–3 , supporting their promise as a scalable cell 35 

therapy for diabetes. However, current protocols still produce immature and heterogeneous SC-islets, 36 

containing incompletely specified pancreatic endocrine cells and off-target states 4. These limitations 37 

indicate that SC-islet differentiation is not yet optimized to produce the cellular identity, maturity, and 38 

function desired for next-generation cell therapy. 39 

Single-cell transcriptomic and chromatin accessibility studies have transformed the characterization of 40 

pancreatic differentiation by resolving cell identities, developmental intermediates, and molecular 41 

differences between in vitro -derived and primary islet cells 5–12 . Yet most single-cell atlases remain 42 

primarily descriptive, making it difficult to translate their insights into actionable interventions. A key 43 

unresolved challenge is predicting which regulatory factors should be perturbed, in which cell state, and 44 

at which developmental window to redirect fate specification 13 . Bridging this gap requires models that 45 

integrate temporal gene-expression and chromatin accessibility dynamics, infer cell-state-specific gene 46 

regulatory networks, and translate these networks into experimentally testable predictions. 47 

Here, we introduce a predictive digital model of human in vitro pancreatic islet differentiation across 48 

multiple cell lines built from temporal single-cell epigenomic and transcriptomic data. This model 49 

transforms descriptive single-cell datasets into experimentally testable predictions of fate-specification 50 

regulators by integrating developmental trajectories with cell-state-specific gene regulatory networks 51 

(GRN). This approach recovers known lineage regulators and identifies previously unreported context-52 

specific transcription factor (TF) activities, including roles for STAT1 and ZEB1 , which we validate in vitro .53 

We also provide an interactive resource to explore predicted regulators and intervention windows across 54 

developmental stages and cell states (https://synbioelab.com/islettwin). Together, this work establishes a 55 

strategy for translating single-cell differentiation datasets into actionable approaches to refine SC-islet 56 

generation. 57

RESULTS 58 

Multiomic mapping of SC-islet differentiation 59 

Differentiation of human pluripotent stem cells into SC-islets relies on sequential exposure to small 60 

molecules and growth factors that mimic key in vivo developmental signals ( Fig. S1A-D ) 14 . This process 61 

yields functional organoids capable of dynamic, biphasic insulin secretion and amelioration of 62 

hyperglycemia in mice ( Fig. S1E-H ). To elucidate cell fate specification during the differentiation process, 63 

we jointly measured gene expression and chromatin accessibility using single-nucleus multiomic 64 

sequencing (snMulti-seq) across 9 time points, spanning 33 days, in 69,535 nuclei ( Fig. 1A and Fig. S2 ). 65 

We resolved 13 developmental cell states based on assessment of canonical marker genes, chromatin 66 

accessibility at regions linked to identity genes, and activity of TF binding motifs over time ( Fig. 1B and 67 

Tables S1-S3 ). 68 

> 69

Global chromatin accessibility peaks during endocrine induction as progenitor-state diversity 70 

expands 71 

Pluripotent cells typically display broad chromatin accessibility that becomes restricted as lineage 72 

potential narrows 15,16 . To evaluate whether analogous trends occur during SC-islet differentiation, we 73 

quantified chromatin accessibility at transcription start sites (TSSs) across gene-associated DNA regions 74 

(Fig. S3A ). We observed increasing accessibility around gene-associated TSSs until day 15, coincident 75 

with the start of endocrine lineage induction ( Fig. 1C ). This pattern suggests an increasingly open 76 

promoter landscape preceding endocrine induction, followed by progressive closing of chromatin as cells 77 

differentiate into more restricted endocrine lineages. 78

chromatin accessibility, and transcription factor motif activity change during SC-islet differentiation. 83

Multiomic trajectory vectors aligned with the expected stagewise progression of SC-islet differentiation. 111

Gene-state analysis reveals context-specific chromatin-transcription regulatory modes 117 

Mapping chromatin accessibility and transcriptional dynamics of DEGs over pseudotime revealed multiple 118 

regulatory modes during SC-islet differentiation ( Fig. 1G and Fig. S5 ). Some genes followed the 119 

expected activation sequence in which chromatin accessibility increased before or alongside gene 120 

expression. This pattern was observed for several endocrine identity genes, including INS , GCG, and 121 

SST . However, other genes deviated from this canonical pattern. SOX17 , a driver of definitive endoderm 122 

identity 36 , maintained chromatin accessibility throughout differentiation despite declining expression, 123 

indicating that transcriptional silencing can occur without immediate chromatin closure. CFTR , a124 

ductal/exocrine marker, remained broadly accessible across multiple cell states but was transcribed 125 

specifically in exocrine cells, suggesting that open chromatin alone is insufficient for expression and that 126 

additional regulatory layers restrict lineage-inappropriate transcription. Together, these examples highlight 127 

that chromatin accessibility and transcription can be coordinated, delayed, or uncoupled depending on 128 

gene and cell-state context. 129 

We characterized these patterns using four gene states 35 : primed (accessibility increases while 130 

expression remains low), coupled-on (accessibility and expression both increase), decoupled 131 

(accessibility and expression change in opposite directions), and coupled-off (accessibility and expression 132 

both decrease) (Fig. S6A and Table S4 ). Genes transitioned between dominant states across 133 

differentiation; therefore, we also defined co-dominant gene states to capture heterogeneous chromatin-134 

transcription dynamics within a cell population ( Fig. S6B ). Most DEGs were classified as coupled-on, 135 

decoupled, or co-dominant for coupled-on and decoupled states, reflecting lineage programs that are 136 

actively induced, maintained, or transcriptionally silenced as trajectories progress. Canonical markers of 137 

pancreatic developmental stages, including SOX17 in DE, SOX9 in PP, and NEUROG3 in early 138

Constructing a predictive digital model of SC-islet differentiation 173 

While our multiomic map provides insight into epigenetic and transcriptional dynamics during SC-islet 174 

differentiation, it does not capture the full breadth of trajectories that can arise across different protocols 175 

and cell lines. Therefore, we developed a framework to construct a more complete digital model of in vitro 176 

differentiation and enable robust downstream in silico analyses ( Fig. 2A ). We integrated 61 individual 177 

single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq), single-nucleus RNA sequencing (snRNA-seq), single-nucleus 178 

ATAC sequencing (snATAC-seq), and snMulti-seq datasets using our multiomic map as an anchor to 179 

generate a shared latent space and enable joint projection ( Fig. 2B-C and Table S5 ). Annotation of the 180 

400,603 cells within the digital model was based on protocol-dependent differentiation day and 181 

expression of canonical marker genes ( Fig. 2D and Figures S7-S13 ). Based on the proportion of cell 182 

states observed across days, we identified six distinct stages that align with those reported by published 183 

protocols ( Fig. 2E ) 37–43 . Stages 1 and 2 are dominated by cells in the DE and PGT states, respectively. In 184 

stage 3, we primarily observe cells in the PP state, followed by a shift to the early EP and late EP cell 185 

states in stage 4. Finally, stages 5 and 6 are characterized by increasing proportions of endocrine cell 186 

states. We reconstructed trajectory dynamics within the digital model by sampling random walks along a 187 

Markov chain constructed from multiomic transition probability matrices, which indicated alpha and beta 188 

cell states as the predominant endpoints ( Fig. 2F and Figure S14 ). This framework provided a highly 189 

accurate model for predicting cell state transitions, with root mean square errors consistently below 0.05 190 

for all cell states across differentiation stages ( Fig. 2G and Table S6 ). 191

In silico transcription factor perturbations identify context-dependent regulators of early 233 

pancreatic progenitor specification 234 

To further interrogate regulatory control during early SC-islet differentiation, we focused on the 235 

unidirectional transition from definitive endoderm to primitive gut tube and pancreatic progenitor states 236 

using 8,684 cells spanning days 5 to 13 from four independent differentiation protocols ( Fig. 3A–B and 237 

Fig. S15A-C ). Using gene expression-based pseudotime, we constructed developmental flow vectors that 238 

captured the trajectory of these early differentiation stages ( Fig. 3C ). We then inferred cell state-specific 239 

gene regulatory networks (GRNs) using an approach that incorporates both gene expression and 240 

chromatin accessibility data at regulatory genomic regions 58 . To summarize network structure, we ranked 241 

transcription factors by degree centrality, which quantifies the number of inferred regulatory connections 242 

and serves as a proxy for potential regulatory activity. Several highly connected transcription factors were 243 

shared across DE, PGT, and PP, including TCF12 , TCF7L2 , and PRDM1 (Fig. S15D ). By contrast, other 244 

transcription factors showed stage-dependent shifts in connectivity, with FOXA1 decreasing and CTCF 245 

increasing as cells progressed from DE to early PP ( Fig. S15D and Table S8 ). 246 

Because GRN connectivity alone is insufficient to infer functional relevance, we simulated 222 individual 247 

transcription factor knockouts and quantified their predicted effects using a perturbation score (PS) 248 

calculated from the inner product of baseline developmental flow vectors and simulated KO vectors ( Fig. 249 

3D-E and Table S9) . KO of ETS2 , CREB3L2 , or BACH1 was predicted to favor PGT specification while 250 

suppressing DE identity ( Fig. 3D ). Conversely, KO of HNF4G or FOS was predicted to promote early PP 251 

specification while suppressing PGT identity ( Fig. 3E ). 252 

We next examined common predicted promoters and inhibitors of PGT and PP development. Predicted 253 

promoters included ID4 , POU2F1 , NR2C2 , and GATA4 , whereas predicted inhibitors included ID2, ID3, 254 

SMC3, and FOXA1. These TF showed distinct degree-centrality trends and scales, suggesting that their 255 

predicted effects were driven by the regulation of specific target genes rather than by overall network 256 

connectivity ( Fig. 3F and Fig. S15E ). Notably, KO of GATA4 was predicted to promote progression 257 

toward both PGT and PP states, despite its established role in endoderm and pancreatic program 258 

induction 17,18 . We therefore examined inferred regulatory coefficients for putative GATA4 targets across 259 

early cell states ( Fig. 3G and Table S10 ). The model predicted that GATA4 positively regulated genes 260

SC-islet differentiation shows persistent transcriptional overactivation relative to fetal in vivo 269 

development 270 

To evaluate how closely in vitro differentiation recapitulates in vivo human pancreatic development, we 271 

integrated five independent scRNA-seq datasets spanning human development over post-conception 272 

weeks 4 to 19 with transcriptomic data from days 10 to 26 of our multiomic map ( Fig. 3H, Fig. S16A-C, 273 

and Table S11 ). The integrated dataset comprised 30,084 cells representing PP, alpha, beta, and delta 274 

cell states ( Fig. 3I and Fig. S16D ). We then performed cell-state-matched DEG analysis between in vivo 275 

development and in vitro differentiation to identify 3,098 significant DEGs between stem cell-derived PPs 276 

(SC-PP) and fetal-derived PPs ( Fig. 3J and Table S12 ), including several transcription factors predicted 277 

by our model to regulate in vitro PP specification. Notably, GATA4 , POU2F1 , and NR2C2 , were 278 

overexpressed in vitro , while ID2, ID3, and ID4 were overexpressed in vivo (Fig. 3K ). 279 

We also observed broad transcriptional overexpression across in vitro EP and terminal endocrine states 280 

compared with matched in vivo states, including a greater than six-fold increase in the number of DEGs in 281 

alpha and beta cells (Fig. S17A-D ). This elevated transcriptional output is consistent with prior 282 

observations that SC-islets retain a more open chromatin landscape and persistent gene regulatory 283 

network activity than primary islets 11,62 . Interestingly, GATA4 was recurrently upregulated across SC-284 

derived states, with elevated expression across EP, alpha, beta, and delta lineages ( Fig. S17E ). These 285 

observations support the in silico prediction that sustained GATA4 activity may constrain optimal in vitro 286

Branch-point modeling identifies regulators of exocrine-endocrine lineage choice 310 

Next, we focused on a subset of 6,726 cells spanning days 11 to 24 from five independent in vitro 311 

differentiation protocols to explore the GRN landscape at the branch point between endocrine and 312 

exocrine lineages from the bipotent PP state ( Fig. 4A and Fig. S18A ). Within this subset, we resolved the 313 

divergent developmental flow from the PP state through two intermediate populations toward either the 314 

exocrine lineage or early EP lineage ( Fig. 4B-C). While we observed decreased expression of PDX1 315 

along both the EP and exocrine lineages, the early EP intermediate population was characterized by 316 

increasing expression of NEUROG3 and CHGA . On the other hand, the exocrine intermediate state had 317 

sustained expression of SOX9 along with increasing expression of CFTR and SPINK1 , which mark ductal 318 

and acinar lineages, respectively ( Fig. S18B-C ). 319 

We next inferred cell state-specific GRNs for each population and ranked TFs by degree centrality to 320 

summarize changes in network connectivity across the two lineages ( Table S13 ). HDAC2 , which encodes 321 

a protein responsible for transcriptional repression and chromatin remodeling 63 , was highly connected 322 

across both lineage trajectories ( Fig. S18D ). This finding aligns with our previous observation that 323 

chromatin accessibility across TSSs begins to decrease genome-wide after day 15 of in vitro 324 

differentiation ( Fig 1D ). We also observed other TFs that had state-specific connectivity patterns ( Fig. 325 

4D ). For example, STAT1 and SOX9 connectivity increased in both intermediate populations, but this 326 

increased connectivity was maintained only along the exocrine lineage. Other TFs showed similar 327 

lineage-specific connectivity changes, with ID1 , EBF1, and ELF1 increasing along the exocrine trajectory, 328 

whereas CTCF and PROX1 increased connectivity along the early EP trajectory. By contrast, TFs such 329 

as ZEB1 showed maximal connectivity in PP and then decreased connectivity along both lineages. 330 

To prioritize TFs that may regulate lineage specification at this branch point, we calculated PS from 331 

systematic in silico TF KO simulations across 226 TFs with inferred regulatory connections in the 332 

intermediate population GRNs ( Fig. 4E and Table S14 ). Many KOs were predicted to promote both 333 

intermediate fates, including KO of ID1 , TCF7L2 , MECOM , FOXA2 , or RXRA (Fig. 4E ). In contrast, the 334 

KO of STAT1 , HDAC2 , or PROX1 was predicted to specifically promote progression toward the 335 

intermediate early EP lineage while suppressing the intermediate exocrine lineage (Fig. S18E ). 336

STAT1 and ZEB1 regulate opposing fates during the exocrine-endocrine lineage specification 343 

We examined the inferred downstream targets of STAT1 , since it has not been previously reported as a 344 

regulator of pancreatic development, to uncover the potential regulatory mechanism underlying the in 345

observation, we inhibited STAT1 transcription using a doxycycline-inducible CRISPR interference 348

construct resulted in a significant decrease in STAT1 expression on day 13 which was reversed by day 350 

26 ( Fig. S18G-H ). On day 26, we also found that the temporary knockdown of STAT1 did not alter 351 

expression of beta cell markers ( INS , NKX6-1 , MAFA ) while significantly reducing expression of exocrine 352

specification, we pharmacologically inhibited JAK–STAT signaling using ruxolitinib (RUXO) over the same 354 

timeframe and again profiled cells on day 26 ( Fig. 4G ). We found that RUXO treatment preserved or 355 

improved expression of beta cell markers, while significantly reducing expression of exocrine markers 356 

(Fig. 4H ). Functional assessment on day 33 demonstrated significantly increased insulin secretion in 357 

response to high glucose stimulation following RUXO treatment ( Fig. 4I ). 358

Lineage-specific GRN modeling of regulators during alpha and delta cell specification 396 

Attempts to control endocrine lineage specification during SC-islet differentiation have had limited 397 

success 65–68 . Therefore, we sought to characterize the GRNs driving alpha and delta cell lineages. To 398 

interrogate regulatory control along alpha lineage specification during in vitro differentiation, we generated 399 

a subset consisting of 16,122 cells from days 10 to 21 ( Fig. S19A-E ). Consistent with previous reports of 400 

multiple alpha cell lineage trajectories 10 , we observed one trajectory progressing from the PP through an 401 

early alpha intermediate state and a second trajectory progressing through the early EP state ( Fig. 402 

S19F ). Inferred GRNs for each population revealed general transcriptional regulators associated with 403 

human development, such as TCF12 and JUND , to be highly connected across all lineage states ( Fig. 404 

S19G and Table S16 ). To identify TFs that may regulate these two alpha cell lineage trajectories, we 405 

performed in silico TF KO simulations for all 227 TFs within this subset. Expectedly, we found that KO of 406 

canonical endocrine and alpha-associated TFs 55–57,69 , such as ARX , MAFB , PAX6 , and NR3C1 , blocked 407 

progression along both alpha trajectories ( Fig. S19H-J and Table S17 ). 408 

We also investigated the regulation of delta cell lineage specification using a subset of 7,583 cells 409 

spanning days 11 to 21 ( Fig. S20A-C ). Within this subset, we observed an intermediate delta state 410 

characterized by lower expression of identity markers, such as SST and HHEX , and slightly higher 411 

expression of NEUROG3 (Fig. S20D-E ). The developmental flow profile revealed a unidirectional 412 

trajectory from PP through EP states into the intermediate delta and delta states ( Fig. S20F ). Inferred 413 

GRNs again revealed TFs associated with broad transcriptional regulation, including TCF12 , JUND , and 414 

HDAC2 , were highly connected across cell states ( Fig. S20G and Table S18 ). In silico TF KO simulations 415 

of the 227 TF in this subset identified PBX3 , FOXO1 , NR2C2 , and NF1 as regulators of delta lineage 416 

progression ( Fig. S20H-J and Table S19 ). These factors have been reported to play diverse roles in 417 

pancreatic development, endocrine function, and somatostatinoma 70–73 . While we did not delve into 418 

specific regulators of the alpha and delta lineages, we provide the inferred regulatory coefficients for 419 

downstream TF targets within the cell-state-specific GRNs ( Tables S20-S21 ) and encourage the use of 420 

our online tool (https://www.synbioelab.com/islettwin) to facilitate future mechanistic studies. 421

DISCUSSION 486 

Understanding how cell fate is regulated during in vitro differentiation is essential for improving SC-islet 487 

cell replacement therapies for diabetes. Although current protocols generate functional endocrine cells, 488 

precise control of endocrine specification remains limited, resulting in off-target populations 65–68 . Here, we 489 

present a predictive digital model of SC-islet differentiation across multiple cell lines that integrates 490 

transcriptional and chromatin accessibility dynamics to reconstruct developmental trajectories, correlate 491 

TF drivers with major cell states, infer cell-state-specific GRNs, and investigate candidate regulators via in 492 

silico TF perturbations. This framework moves beyond a descriptive atlas of in vitro differentiation by 493 

providing an interactive model of regulatory control across DE-to-PP progression, exocrine-endocrine 494 

bifurcation, and terminal endocrine lineage decisions. 495 

A major insight from our multiomic assessment is that SC-islet differentiation does not follow the expected 496 

trend in which chromatin accessibility progressively decreases as cells transition from immature 497 

progenitors to terminal endocrine states 16,74 . Instead, chromatin accessibility across TSSs increased until 498 

the endocrine induction window, coinciding with divergent developmental flow and enrichment of primed 499 

gene states in EP cells. These findings suggest that chromatin accessibility reflects the differentiation 500 

potential of progenitor populations and can transiently increase before multi-lineage specification events. 501 

We also found that gene expression, chromatin accessibility, and TF motif activity can be temporally 502 

coupled or uncoupled depending on gene and cell-state context, consistent with our previous comparative 503 

analyses 11,62 . While some genes followed the expected pattern in which chromatin opening preceded or 504 

accompanied transcriptional activation, others remained accessible despite transcriptional silencing or 505 

were expressed only in specific lineages despite broader accessibility. Thus, chromatin accessibility alone 506 

is insufficient to infer transcriptional output, and vice versa 75 , highlighting the value of paired snMulti-seq 507 

measurements for building integrated multiomic GRNs 76,77 .508 

The digital model also enabled comparison between in vitro differentiation and human pancreatic 509 

development in vivo . Although SC-islet differentiation captured major developmental transitions, it did not 510 

fully recapitulate the extent or timing of transcriptional repression observed during fetal pancreatic 511 

development. In vitro -derived pancreatic progenitor and endocrine states showed broad transcriptional 512

It is made The copyright holder for this preprint this version posted July 16, 2026. ;https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.04.27.721124 doi: bioRxiv preprint

overexpression relative to matched fetal states, including recurrent overexpression of GATA4 across 513 

multiple SC-derived lineages. This finding contextualizes the in silico prediction that sustained high 514 

GATA4 activity may constrain early pancreatic specification, despite the established requirement for 515 

GATA4 to develop the pancreas 17,18,78 . More broadly, our observations indicate that in vitro differentiation 516 

is not an accelerated version of fetal development, but a related process with distinct regulatory 517 

mechanisms and lineage-state constraints. 518 

Simulated TF perturbations further demonstrate the importance of context-specific regulatory activity. The 519 

model recovered expected roles for established pancreatic regulators, including FOXA2 , SOX9 ,520 

NEUROG3 , RFX6 , ARX , PDX1 , and NKX6-1 , 19,22,44,49,52,57,79 supporting the biological validity of the 521 

inferred GRNs. At the exocrine-endocrine branch point, in silico TF KO simulations suggested roles for 522 

STAT1 and ZEB1 as lineage regulators with opposing lineage effects. In vitro studies supported these 523 

predictions with transient STAT1 inhibition resulting in reduced exocrine marker expression and improved 524 

glucose-stimulated insulin secretion, whereas early ZEB1 knockdown impaired endocrine specification. 525 

During downstream beta cell specification, however, in silico simulations suggested ZEB1 activity 526 

promoted the off-target SIC fate, which was validated by in vitro ZEB1 knockdown resulting in reduced 527 

SIC markers and serotonin content while increasing the proportion of beta cells. These results illustrate 528 

that TF function is not fixed, but depends on developmental timing, cell state, and target-gene context. 529 

Overall, this work establishes a predictive framework for translating single-cell multiomic data into 530 

experimentally testable strategies to improve in vitro differentiation efficiency. By integrating temporal 531 

multiomic measurements with GRN inference and in silico perturbation, the model identifies conserved 532 

and context-specific regulators of pancreatic lineage specification. More broadly, this study illustrates how 533 

predictive models can build on single-cell characterization of stem cell differentiation by connecting cell-534 

state maps to testable regulatory hypotheses, rational strategies to refine cell identity, reduced off-target 535 

fate specification, and improved future cell replacement therapies for diabetes. 536

snMulti-seq data processing 1355 

snMulti-seq data were processed and aligned to the human reference genome assembly 1356 

GRCh38 using Cell Ranger ARC (v2.0). A matrix of peaks per cell was constructed for each sample 1357 

based on ATAC-seq fragments measured per sample across a common peak set generated from all 1358 

samples. Initial quality control was performed on each sample individually to remove low quality cells 1359 

based on the total number of RNA molecules detected per cell, total number of ATAC peaks per cell, 1360 

approximate ratio of mononucleosomal to nucleosome-free fragments (nucleosome signal), and 1361 

transcriptional start site enrichment (TSSe) score. We removed nuclei with more than 40,000 RNA 1362 

molecules or ATAC peaks per cell. Nuclei with less than 1,000 RNA molecules or ATAC peaks per cell 1363 

were also removed. Nuclei with a TSSe score less than 2 were removed. Nuclei with a nucleosome signal 1364 

greater than 1.5 were removed, except for the Day 26 and Day 33 samples where the filter threshold was 1365 

set to 5. Final peak calling was performed using Model-based Analysis of ChIP-Seq (MACS2) 80 . The 1366 

standard Seurat preprocessing workflow was used to normalize, scale, and center RNA data 81 . Linear 1367 

dimensional reduction was performed by principal component analysis, as implemented in Seurat. 1368 

Normalization and linear dimensional reduction of ATAC data was performed using term frequency-1369 

inverse document frequency (TF-IDF) and singular value decomposition (SVD) functions, respectively, as 1370 

implemented in Signac 76 . Joint Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection (UMAP) of RNA and 1371 

ATAC data was constructed based on a weighted nearest neighbor (WNN) calculated from both RNA and 1372 

ATAC dimensional reductions. 1373
