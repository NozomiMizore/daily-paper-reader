## 摘要
单细胞多模态组学提供了前所未有的细胞网络分辨率，但将连续的计算机归因转化为结构化、可测试的生物学机制仍然是一个持续存在的瓶颈。为解决这一限制，我们引入了一种分析流程，采用决策树将连续的神经网络归因离散化为明确的调控阈值。这些边界随后在结构上约束大语言模型，使其能够将已有文献与经验数据整合，以合成上下文特定的假设。将该连续到离散的框架应用于稀疏数据集，产生了新的生物学机制。具体而言，该框架阐述了调控EGF刺激途径的细胞骨架门控层级，识别了皮质中间神经元输入电阻的转录组驱动因素，并描绘了在空间转录组学中预测Ki-67丰度的翻译逻辑。回顾性基准测试验证了该框架自主重建已发表调控逻辑的能力。在本地可部署的开权重语言模型和无代码界面的支持下，该方法建立了一种可审计的方法，从高维单细胞数据中提取稳健的实验假设。

## Abstract
Single-cell multimodal omics offer unprecedented resolution of cellular networks, yet translating continuous computational attributions into structured, testable biological mechanisms remains a persistent bottleneck. To address this limitation, we introduce an analytical pipeline employing decision trees to discretize continuous neural network attributions into explicit regulatory thresholds. These boundaries then structurally constrain large language models, enabling them to integrate established literature with empirical data to synthesize context-specific hypotheses. Applying this continuous-to-discrete framework across sparse datasets yielded novel biological mechanisms. Specifically, the framework articulated a cytoskeletal gating hierarchy governing EGF-stimulated pathways, identified transcriptomic drivers of input resistance in cortical interneurons, and delineated translational logic predicting Ki-67 abundance within spatial transcriptomics. Retrospective benchmarking validated the frameworks capacity to autonomously reconstruct published regulatory logic. Supported by a locally deployable open-weight language model and a code-free interface, this approach establishes an auditable methodology to extract robust experimental hypotheses from high-dimensional single-cell data.
