## 摘要
为了加深对药物在生理相关系统中作用机制的理解，我们开发了一个高通量的原代人细胞类型化合物反应转录组图谱。利用可扩展且经济的 Digital RNA with pertUrbation of Genes（DRUG-seq）技术，我们在四种不同的原代细胞类型（主动脉平滑肌细胞、骨骼肌成肌细胞、皮肤成纤维细胞和黑色素细胞）中，对89种具有药理活性的化合物在六个浓度梯度下的基因表达反应进行了分析。通过严格的质量控制和标准化，我们生成了可重复且具有细胞类型分辨率的转录组特征，从而能够发现共享和差异性的调控程序。该数据集揭示了核心细胞反应，例如布雷菲德菌素A在所有细胞类型中诱导的内质网应激，以及谱系特异性效应，包括地塞米松在主动脉平滑肌细胞中诱导的低氧信号、与上皮-间充质转化通路相关的骨骼肌成肌细胞复杂炎症反应、转化生长因子-β在成纤维细胞中调节的状态，以及达拉非尼在黑色素细胞中驱动向静止状态的转录转变。通过将系统性扰动与原代模型相结合，该数据集为构建药物反应和机制的系统级模型提供了资源。最终，我们旨在通过基于人类生物学的高通量数据生成，并可直接用于人工智能模型，来加速预测药理学的发展。

## Abstract
To advance our understanding of drug action in physiologically-relevant systems, we developed a high-throughput transcriptomic atlas of compound responses in primary human cell types. Leveraging the scalable and cost-effective Digital RNA with the pertUrbation of Genes (DRUG-seq) assay, we profiled gene expression responses to 89 pharmacologically-active compounds across six concentrations in four distinct primary cell types: aortic smooth muscle cells (AoSMCs), skeletal muscle myoblasts (SkMMs), dermal fibroblasts, and melanocytes. Through rigorous quality control and normalization, we generated reproducible and cell type-resolved transcriptomic signatures, enabling the discovery of both shared and divergent regulatory programs. This dataset revealed core cellular responses, such as brefeldin A-mediated ER stress across all cell types, as well as lineage-specific effects, including dexamethasone-induced hypoxia signaling in AoSMCs, complex inflammatory responses linked to epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition pathways in SkMMs, TGF-{beta}-modulated states in fibroblasts, and dabrafenib-driven transcriptional shifts towards quiescence in melanocytes. By integrating systematic perturbations with primary models, this dataset serves as a resource for building systems-level models of drug response and mechanism. Ultimately, we aim to accelerate predictive pharmacology by enabling high-throughput data generation grounded in human biology and readily usable by artificial intelligence models.
